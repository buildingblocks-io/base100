# Afterword

## What to do next

If BASE-100 did its job, you now share one **coordinate system** for maturity: ten domains, ten levels, evidence-led language, and a score that compresses complexity without pretending the business is simple.

**Pick one constraint.** The framework is broad on purpose; improvement is sequential. Identify the weakest domain or level that actually limits the next stage of scale, predictability, or transferability—and work it until the evidence moves.

**Keep the standard in the room.** Revisit definitions when leadership changes, when you enter a new scale band, or when the Institute publishes a new revision. Maturity is not a one-off project; it is a **discipline**.

**Close the loop.** Audit questions in this book are designed to produce observable answers. Capture them, date them, and compare the next pass. That is how a standard stops being literature and becomes infrastructure.

Thank you for reading. Build the asset.
