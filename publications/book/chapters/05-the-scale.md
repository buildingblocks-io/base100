# The Scale

## Measuring Maturity

Maturity is not about size. A larger company can be less mature than a smaller one.

### Levels 1 to 3: Defined to Owned

The company is still founder-led. The basic object, process, or rule exists, but it remains fragile.

### Levels 4 to 6: Standardised to Proactive

The business gains operational order. Work is repeatable, measurable, and increasingly preventive rather than reactive.

### Levels 7 to 9: Integrated to Independent

The domains work together, improve through feedback, and become less dependent on key individuals.

### Level 10: Transferable

The domain is highly autonomous, governable, and ready for institutional oversight or external transfer.

A high total score does not cancel out a critical weakness. Maturity still depends on the weakest important constraint.
